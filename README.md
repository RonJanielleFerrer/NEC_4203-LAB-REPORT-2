# Experiment 9 – Frequency Modulation (FM)

## Objective
The objective of this experiment is to study frequency modulation by observing how a message signal changes the frequency of a carrier.

## Materials and Components Used
- Emona Telecoms-Trainer 101
- Oscilloscope
- Patch leads

## Circuit / Block Diagram
The setup uses a voltage-controlled oscillator (VCO) to produce FM signals.

## Procedure / Experimental Setup
1. A message signal was applied to the VCO input.
2. Carrier frequency deviations were observed.
3. Modulation index was adjusted and analyzed.

## Theory and Concepts
In FM, information is encoded by varying the carrier frequency rather than amplitude. This technique provides improved noise immunity.

## Results and Observations
Frequency deviation was visible as changes in waveform spacing. Increasing message amplitude increased deviation.

## Conclusion
The experiment successfully demonstrated FM signal generation.

## Reflection / Learning Summary
This experiment improved my understanding of FM behavior and its advantages over AM systems.

# Experiment 10 – FM Demodulation

## Objective
The objective of this experiment is to recover the original message signal from a frequency-modulated carrier.

## Materials and Components Used
- Emona Telecoms-Trainer 101
- Oscilloscope
- Patch leads

## Circuit / Block Diagram
The FM demodulator uses filtering and frequency-to-voltage conversion.

## Procedure / Experimental Setup
1. An FM signal was generated.
2. The demodulator circuit was connected.
3. Output signals were observed and compared with the original message.

## Theory and Concepts
FM demodulation converts frequency variations into voltage changes, reconstructing the original message signal.

## Results and Observations
The recovered signal closely matched the original message with minimal distortion.

## Conclusion
FM demodulation was successfully achieved.

## Reflection / Learning Summary
I learned how receiver circuits recover transmitted information. Proper tuning was critical to minimize distortion.
